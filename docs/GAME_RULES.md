# WHOT Game Rules

## Overview
WHOT is a Nigerian card game played with 2-4 players. The goal is to be the first player to play all cards from your hand.

## Card Deck

The WHOT deck contains:

### Numbered Cards (1-19)
- **Circle (Green)**: 5 copies (1-19)
- **Triangle (Blue)**: 5 copies (1-19)
- **Square (Red)**: 5 copies (1-19)
- **Star (Yellow)**: 5 copies (1-19)
- **Cross (Purple)**: 5 copies (1-19)

### Special Cards
- **WHOT 20**: 4 copies - Player chooses a shape
- **Hold On**: 15 copies (3 per shape) - Skip next player
- **Pick Two**: 15 copies (3 per shape) - Next player picks 2 cards
- **General Market**: 15 copies (3 per shape) - All players pick 1 card
- **Suspension**: 15 copies (3 per shape) - Block next player
- **Star**: 15 copies (3 per shape) - Special action

**Total Cards: 110**

## Game Setup

1. Each player is dealt 4 cards
2. The remaining cards form the draw pile
3. One card is placed face-up as the starting card
4. If the starting card is special, reshuffle and draw again

## Game Flow

### Player Turn
On your turn, you must:

1. **Play a valid card** from your hand, OR
2. **Pick a card** from the deck if you can't play

### Valid Card Play
You can play a card if:
- The card's **shape matches** the top card's shape, OR
- The card's **number matches** the top card's number
- **Exception**: WHOT card (20) can always be played

### After Shape Selection (WHOT 20)
If you play a WHOT 20 card:
1. You choose a shape (Circle, Triangle, Square, Star, or Cross)
2. The next player must play a card of that shape
3. Or they can play another WHOT card

## Special Cards Effects

### Hold On
- Next player's turn is skipped
- Play continues to the following player

### Pick Two
- Next player must pick 2 cards from the draw pile
- They lose their turn
- Play continues to the following player

### General Market
- ALL players pick 1 card from the draw pile
- Including the player who played the card
- Play continues normally

### Suspension
- Next player's turn is blocked
- Similar to "Hold On"

### Star
- Special action card
- May have various effects based on house rules

## Winning

**A player wins when:**
- They play their last card
- Their hand becomes empty

## Special Rules

### Draw Pile Empty
- If the draw pile is empty when a player needs to pick cards:
  1. Shuffle the discard pile (except top card)
  2. Use shuffled pile as new draw pile
  3. Continue game

### Consecutive WHOT Cards
- Multiple WHOT cards can be played in sequence
- Each player chooses their own shape

### Direction
- Game plays clockwise by default
- Can change direction with certain rules

## Scoring (For Money Matches)

### 2 Players
- Winner: 80% of prize pool
- Platform Fee: 20%

### 3 Players
- 1st Place: 60% of prize pool
- 2nd Place: 40% of prize pool
- Platform Fee: Not applicable

### 4 Players
- 1st Place: 60% of prize pool
- 2nd Place: 40% of prize pool
- 3rd & 4th Place: No reward
- Platform Fee: 20%

## Tournament Rules

### Entry
- Players pay entry fee
- Must join before tournament starts

### Rounds
- Multiple matches are played
- Winners advance to next round
- Format depends on player count

### Finals
- Top players compete in final match
- Winner determined by last player standing

### Prize Distribution
- Platform Fee: 30%
- Remaining pool distributed to winners
- Specific amounts set by tournament creator

## Platform Rules

### Fair Play
- All card dealing done by server
- Move validation on server-side
- Anti-cheat system prevents invalid moves

### Disconnection
- If player disconnects:
  - They have 2 minutes to reconnect
  - After 2 minutes, they forfeit
  - Game continues with remaining players

### Timeout
- Each player has 30 seconds per turn
- After 30 seconds, player automatically picks a card
- Turn passes to next player

### Penalties
- Cheating attempts result in account suspension
- Repeated violations lead to permanent ban

## Tips for Winning

1. **Remember played cards** - Track what's been played
2. **Plan ahead** - Think about your next moves
3. **Use special cards wisely** - Time your special cards strategically
4. **Watch opponents** - Count their cards and predict plays
5. **WHOT strategy** - Choose shapes that help you later
6. **Conservative play** - Don't rush to play cards
7. **Draw strategically** - Sometimes picking a card helps long-term
